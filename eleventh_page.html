Birth Of CHONKS first follower, Moses

In the Book of Exodus, it is recounted that the Israelites were enslaved in Egypt. The Pharaoh of Egypt ordered that all Israelite male infants be killed, as he feared the growing number of Israelites. Moses' mother, Jochebed, gave birth to a son during this time. To protect him from Pharaoh's decree, she hid him for three months. When it became impossible to conceal him any longer, she placed the baby in a basket made of bulrushes and placed it among the reeds by the bank of the Nile River. Moses' older sister, Miriam, watched over the baby from a distance. Coincidentally, Pharaoh's daughter came to the river to bathe and found the basket with the baby inside. She felt compassion for the child and decided to adopt him as her own. Miriam, who had been watching, offered to find a nurse for the baby, and Pharaoh's daughter agreed. Miriam brought her mother, Jochebed, to nurse Moses, so he grew up in Pharaoh's palace as a member of the royal family. This act of saving Moses from certain death was crucial, as he would go on to become a central figure in the liberation of the Israelites from Egyptian slavery and receive the Ten Commandments from God on Mount Sinai. Moses' life played a significant role in the history of the Israelites and their journey to freedom.
